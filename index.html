About me

I am a web application developer at UC Davis who enjoys building mobile apps in my spare time
I noticed that the app market is short of educational Arabic teaching apps for children, so I am
trying to fill this gap and do my part in utilizing new technology to help benefit the children
with something that will boost their knowledge and entertain them at the same time.

Mobile Apps

Lease Miles Widget

A simple minimal app that calculates the mileage that your lease car should be reading at any day.
You just need to input the start date, the initial mileage, and the daily mileage allowance.
The app has a widget for both homescreen and lockscreen (for devices with JB 4.2).

Ashkal wa Alwan

Easy way to learn the Arabic colors and shapes (الألوان والأشكال) for kids optimized for both phones and tablets. The interface is rather simple with shapes that can change colors, and example real life objects for those shapes

Abjad

This is a very useful app for teaching the Arabic letters for preschoolers. It has the following activities:

  • The alphabet song showing each letter as it is pronounced
  • A table of all the letters where a short song plays showing the letter pressed followed by a word that starts with that letter
  • A simple test that basically shows a picture and the child has to click on the appropriate letter that corresponds to the picture displayed
  • A game to connect the letters to their corresponding image
  • Connect the dots to draw the letters: a fun way to teach the child how to write the letters
